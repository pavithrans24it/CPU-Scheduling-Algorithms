Shortest Job First (SJF) Scheduling

Algorithm Explanation

Shortest Job First (SJF) is a CPU scheduling algorithm where the process with the smallest burst time is executed first.

Key Characteristics:

  • Non-preemptive: A process cannot be interrupted once it starts
  • Burst time is known in advance
  • Minimizes average waiting time
  • Can cause starvation of longer processes
  • Optimal for minimizing average waiting time

Advantages:

  • Minimum average waiting time
  • Optimal scheduling algorithm

Disadvantages:

  • Difficult to know burst time in advance
  • Can starve long jobs
