First Come First Serve (FCFS) Scheduling

Algorithm Explanation

First Come First Serve (FCFS) is the simplest CPU scheduling algorithm. Processes are executed in the order they arrive in the ready queue.

Key Characteristics:

  • Non-preemptive: A process cannot be interrupted once it starts
  • FIFO (First In First Out) based
  • Easy to implement and understand
  • Can lead to high average waiting time (convoy effect)
  • Not suitable for interactive systems

Formula:

  • Waiting Time of first process = 0
  • Waiting Time of next process = Waiting Time of previous + Burst Time of previous
  • Turnaround Time = Waiting Time + Burst Time
